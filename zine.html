About the Project

Flawless Hacks is a non-profit organization committed to supporting women and non-binary people in tech. We are extending our programming to create a zine in hopes of giving a platform to traditionally unheard voices in tech and also present a diverse set of experiences.


For our first issue, “Our Tech Journey,” we are asking people to submit art that explores their early relationships with technology and how that’s developed over time. We also welcome submissions that explore the first time you felt interested in or passionate about tech.


We draw inspiration from illustrating Flawless Hacks organizers’ stories (Nicole Zhu, Radhika Mattoo, Dana Lee, Jessica Joseph) and articles such as “'Neopets': Inside Look at Early 2000s Internet Girl Culture” that explore the ways people have become interested in technology. Some additional examples of early tech culture include online communities on sites such as Gaia Online, DeviantArt, MySpace etc. and devices such as GameBoy, Tamagochi, etc.


Submission Guidelines

If you’d like to submit, fill out this form! All are welcome to submit. We are accepting submissions in the following creative formats:

  • Photography
  • Art/illustration

Compensation

We will compensate each submission $30 + a physical copy of the zine.

We are a non-profit organization and will use the proceeds from the zine to fund Flawless Hacks programming.
